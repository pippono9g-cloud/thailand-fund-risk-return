Net inflow (above dashed line) Net outflow (below dashed line) Bubble size = current AUM (B THB)  ·  dashed line = AUM change explained by return alone (above it ≈ net inflow)
Implication — a category above the dashed line is drawing net inflows (new money chasing the theme — momentum / investor conviction); below it is seeing net outflows (redemptions or rotation out) even when returns are positive; sitting on the line means AUM simply tracked performance with flat flows. The farther a bubble sits from the line, the stronger the flow signal — and a category that is both high-return and well above the line is where performance and demand reinforce each other.
Methodology
  • Each bubble is an AIMC sub-category.
  • X = median total return of all share classes in the category over the selected window (from the weekly SEC build).
  • Y = % change in the category's total AUM over the same window, from SEC NAV history. Only share classes with AUM at both window endpoints are counted, so the change is like-for-like.
  • AUM change ≈ market move + net flows, so the vertical distance from the dashed 45° line ≈ net subscription (above the line) or redemption (below).
  • Implied flow = AUM change − median return, in percentage points (pp): +5 pp ≈ net inflow of about 5% of the category's assets; −5 pp ≈ net outflow. (It's "pp" not "%" because it's the gap between two percentages.)
  • Categories with fewer than 3 funds are excluded.
